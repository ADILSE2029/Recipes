BHINDI

Description

Bhindi, also known as okra or lady's finger, is a popular vegetable in South Asian cooking. It has a mild, slightly earthy taste and a distinctive texture that becomes tender when cooked properly. Bhindi masala is a simple stir-fried dish made by cooking okra with onions, tomatoes, and a blend of spices, commonly served with roti or rice as part of everyday meals in Pakistani and Indian households.

Ingredients

  • 250g bhindi (okra), washed and chopped
  • 1 onion, sliced
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 tsp ginger garlic paste
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • 1/2 tsp coriander powder
  • Salt to taste
  • 3 tbsp cooking oil

Steps

  1. Heat oil in a pan and add sliced onions, cooking until golden.
  2. Add ginger garlic paste and cook for a minute.
  3. Add chopped tomatoes and cook until soft.
  4. Add turmeric, red chili powder, coriander powder, and salt, then mix well.
  5. Add the chopped bhindi and stir to coat with the spice mixture.
  6. Cover and cook on low heat for 12 to 15 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the bhindi is tender.
  7. Serve hot with roti or rice.
