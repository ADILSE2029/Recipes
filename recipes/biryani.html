Biryani

Description

Biryani is a rich, flavorful rice dish made by layering marinated meat with spiced basmati rice, then cooking them together so the flavors blend through steam and slow heat. It typically includes whole spices, fried onions, and sometimes potatoes, and is considered one of the most popular festive and everyday dishes across Pakistan and South Asia.

Ingredients

  • 500g basmati rice, soaked for 30 minutes
  • 500g chicken or beef, cut into pieces
  • 2 onions, sliced and fried
  • 1 cup yogurt
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 2 tbsp ginger garlic paste
  • 2 tsp biryani masala
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • Salt to taste
  • Whole spices (cinnamon, cloves, cardamom)
  • 4 tbsp cooking oil
  • A few strands of saffron soaked in milk

Steps

  1. Marinate the meat with yogurt, ginger garlic paste, and spices for at least 30 minutes.
  2. Heat oil and fry onions until golden, then set aside.
  3. In the same oil, cook the marinated meat with tomatoes until tender and the gravy thickens.
  4. Boil the soaked rice separately with whole spices until about 70 percent cooked, then drain.
  5. Layer the partially cooked rice over the meat gravy in the same pot.
  6. Top with fried onions and saffron milk.
  7. Cover tightly and cook on low heat for 15 to 20 minutes to let the layers steam together.
  8. Gently mix before serving hot.
